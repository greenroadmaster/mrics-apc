Develop and maintain realistic budgets; cost planning at project stages; monitoring actual spend against forecast; cost control and variance management.

**Evidence:**

- **Supporting:** Budget approval memo (signed by client, dated March 2024)
- **Total Baseline:** RM 96.8M (excludes contingency, escalation)

**Assessor Commentary:**
> "Clear budget structure enables monthly variance tracking. WBS alignment ensures cost control at activity level."

---

#### 2B. Cost Loading to Schedule (EVM Baseline)
**What I Did:**
- Loaded budget into Primavera P6 schedule (time-phased)
- Created cost histogram showing monthly projected spend
- Aligned cost curve with project milestone schedule
- Established BCWS (Budgeted Cost of Work Scheduled) baseline

**Evidence:**
- **Document:** `Primavera-P6-WBS-OBS-setup.png` (cost loading screenshot)
- **Supporting:** Schedule baseline report (400+ activities with budget allocation)
- **Cost Curve:** S-curve showing projected monthly spend Jan 2024 – Sep 2026

**Assessor Commentary:**
> "Time-phased budget enables early warning of cost/schedule impacts. Professional EVM baseline setup."

---

#### 2C. Monthly Cost Tracking & EVM Metrics
**What I Did:**
- Collected actual cost data from subcontractor invoices & IPCs each month
- Calculated EVM metrics: BCWS (Budgeted), BCWP (Actual %), ACWP (Actual Cost)
- Computed performance indices: CPI = BCWP/ACWP, SPI = BCWP/BCWS
- Produced monthly EVM report for management

**Evidence:**
- **Document:** `EVM-dashboard-monthly.xlsx` (25 months of data, May 2024 – May 2025)
- **Metrics Sample (May 2025):**
  - BCWS: RM 95.2M
  - BCWP: RM 93.1M
  - ACWP: RM 95.0M
  - CPI: 0.98 (slightly over budget, acceptable)
  - SPI: 0.98 (slightly behind schedule, acceptable)

**Assessor Commentary:**
> "Rigorous monthly EVM tracking. CPI/SPI indices demonstrate disciplined cost & schedule control. Professional reporting format."

---

#### 2D. Cost Variance Analysis
**What I Did:**
- Calculated monthly cost variance: ACWP vs BCWS
- Identified variances >5% for root cause analysis
- Categorized variances: Design changes, scope additions, rate escalations, productivity
- Prepared corrective action recommendations

**Evidence:**
- **Document:** `cost-variance-analysis-YTD.pdf` (May 2025 report)
- **Variance Breakdown:**
  - Unfavorable (over budget): Labor escalation, design changes = +RM 2.1M (2.2%)
  - Favorable (under budget): Subcontractor competition, efficiency = -RM 1.8M (1.9%)
  - **Net Variance:** +RM 0.3M (0.3% — acceptable control)

**Assessor Commentary:**
> "Detailed variance analysis with root cause identification. Shows understanding of cost drivers and control mechanisms."

---

#### 2E. Cost Forecasting (EAC & Forecast at Completion)
**What I Did:**
- Used EVM data to calculate EAC (Estimate at Completion)
- Projected final cost based on actual burn rate + remaining scope
- Updated EAC monthly as new data became available
- Flagged cost risks early if EAC exceeded budget

**Evidence:**
- **Document:** `P6-EAC-forecast-report.pdf` (May 2025)
- **EAC Calculation:**
  - Current ACWP: RM 95.0M (85% progress)
  - CPI = 0.98, therefore EAC = BAC / CPI = RM 96.8M / 0.98 = **RM 98.8M**
  - Variance at Completion (VAC) = BAC – EAC = –RM 2.0M
  - **Interpretation:** Project forecast to overrun by RM 2.0M (2.1%)

**Assessor Commentary:**
> "Professional EAC calculation using CPI method. Early visibility of cost exposure enables corrective action planning."

---

#### 2F. Subcontractor Payment Schedule & Cash Flow
**What I Did:**
- Created payment schedule for 15 subcontractors based on contract terms
- Tracked scheduled vs actual payments
- Projected monthly cash outflows for 24-month project duration
- Coordinated payment releases with client finance team

**Evidence:**
- **Document:** `subcontractor-payment-schedule.xlsx`
- **Typical Payment Terms:** 30 days net (contractor invoices to client payment)
- **Payment Control:** All payments aligned with certified IPC (no advance payment)
- **Cash Flow Projection:** Cumulative spending curve from RM 0 to RM 101.8M

**Assessor Commentary:**
> "Professional payment management. Linking payment to IPC certification demonstrates cost control discipline."

---

#### 2G. Contingency & Escalation Management
**What I Did:**
