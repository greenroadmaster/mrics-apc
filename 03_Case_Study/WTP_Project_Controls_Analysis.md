## 1. Executive Summary: Project Context

### 1.1 Project Overview

The Water Treatment Plant is a critical utility infrastructure project supporting a 20MW AI Data Center. The scope includes design, procurement, civil works, equipment installation, and system commissioning over a 29-week delivery period.

**Key Characteristics:**
- **Capital Value:** USD 3.59M
- **Complexity:** Medium (mixed civil & MEP)
- **Risk Profile:** Medium-High (ground conditions, equipment procurement)
- **Stakeholders:** Data center owner, utilities authority, environmental regulator
- **Quality Requirement:** Operational readiness at commissioning

### 1.2 Why This Case Study Demonstrates Project Controls?

This project exemplifies the integration of:
- **Cost Planning:** Bottom-up estimate from detailed scope breakdown
- **Schedule Management:** Critical path analysis with resource constraints
- **Risk Assessment:** Identification of high-impact activities
- **Performance Measurement:** Earned value framework
- **Value Engineering:** Cost vs. schedule trade-off analysis

**Phase 6 (Commissioning): Analogy-Based**
- Similar data center projects: 4-6% of total construction cost
- Performance testing: 2-3% additional

### 2.2 Cost Control Plan

#### 2.2.1 Variance Analysis Framework

**Earned Value Management (EVM) Metrics:**

$$\text{Cost Variance (CV)} = \text{Earned Value (EV)} - \text{Actual Cost (AC)}$$

$$\text{Cost Performance Index (CPI)} = \frac{\text{EV}}{\text{AC}}$$

**Target CPI:** > 0.95 (acceptable range: 0.90-1.05)

#### 2.2.2 Monthly Cost Tracking

| Month | Phase | Budget | Actual | Variance | CPI | Comment |
|-------|-------|--------|--------|----------|-----|---------|

| M14-16 | Startup & Testing | USD 185K | TBD | — | — | Final handover |

#### 2.2.3 Cost Control Thresholds

- **Threshold:** ±5% variance
- **Yellow Flag:** 2-5% overrun → investigate & correct
- **Red Flag:** >5% overrun → change control required
- **Green:** Within tolerance → proceed as planned

### 4.2 Cost-Schedule Trade-off Analysis

**Scenario 1: Accelerated Schedule (Target: Week 26 instead of 29)**

| Change | Cost Impact | Schedule Impact | Feasibility |
|--------|------------|-----------------|-------------|
| +1 shift concrete work | +USD 80,000 | -1 week | Moderate (labor cost) |

**Scenario 2: Extended Schedule (Target: Week 33 instead of 29)**

| Change | Cost Impact | Schedule Impact | Benefit |
|--------|------------|-----------------|---------|

## 6. Value Engineering & Optimization

### 6.1 Value Engineering Opportunities

#### Opportunity 1: Equipment Standardization
**Current State:** Custom tank designs  
**Proposal:** Use pre-fabricated modular units  
**Benefit:** 
- Cost saving: USD 80,000 (12% reduction in equipment)
- Schedule saving: 2 weeks (installation speed)
- Trade-off: Reduced flexibility for future expansion

**Recommendation:** ACCEPT (standard design sufficient)

#### Opportunity 2: Waterproofing Method
**Current State:** 2-layer bituminous + PVC membrane  
**Proposal:** Single-layer polyurethane spray coating  
**Benefit:**
- Cost saving: USD 40,000 (membrane cost)
- Labor saving: 1 week (faster application)
- Risk: Durability history less proven

**Recommendation:** CONDITIONAL (test on sample area first)

#### Opportunity 3: Commissioning Approach
**Current State:** Full system simultaneous startup  
**Proposal:** Phased startup (aeration → clarification → filtration → disinfection)  
**Benefit:**
- Risk reduction: 80% (easier troubleshooting)
- Time benefit: No change (sequential doesn't extend duration)
- Cost: USD 15,000 additional (extended vendor support)

**Recommendation:** ACCEPT (risk reduction worth USD 15K)

#### Opportunity 4: Concrete Supplier Strategy
**Current State:** Single supplier  
**Proposal:** Dual suppliers (50/50 split)  
**Benefit:**
- Risk reduction: Supply/quality competition
- Cost: Potential 5-8% savings (competitive pricing)
- Trade-off: Quality control complexity

**Recommendation:** ACCEPT if suppliers pre-qualified

## 9. Lessons Learned & Best Practices

### 9.1 Key Success Factors

1. **Early Design Completion** - Reduces downstream schedule risk
2. **Procurement Pre-ordering** - Critical for Week 2-7 delivery windows
3. **Geotechnical Investigation** - Prevents piling cost overruns
4. **Phased Commissioning** - Reduces system integration risk by 80%
5. **Integrated Cost-Schedule** - Enables informed trade-off decisions

### 9.2 Risks Lessons

**From this project:**
- Equipment lead times drive critical path (35-day minimum)
- Weather risk on concrete work (allow 2-week contingency)
- Ground conditions are top cost driver (budget contingency)
- Commissioning takes 5-7% of project duration (plan accordingly)
